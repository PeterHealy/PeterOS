# Ben Alistor Content Rules LinkedIn Post

## What This Source Is

A short LinkedIn post offering three practical filters for deciding whether a piece of content is strong enough to publish.

## Key Ideas

- Strong content should have a clear point of view rather than sounding generic.
- Content should feel conversational and relational rather than preachy or one-directional.
- The hook should tell the audience what value or outcome they are about to get.

## Claims Worth Tracking

- A simple pre-publication checklist can materially improve content quality.
- Distinctiveness, conversational tone, and clarity of promised outcome are useful filters across social content, not just LinkedIn.
- Many weak posts fail not because the topic is wrong, but because the framing is generic or the hook hides the value.

## Caveats

- This is a concise heuristics post, not a full strategy framework.
- The accessible capture is partial and based on search result text.
- These rules are best used as quality filters, not as a full content system.
