# How Id Promote My Startup If I Had 0 Followers

## What This Source Is

A tactical bootstrap-distribution post arguing that a startup with no audience should borrow existing attention by intercepting competitor mentions, posting inside founder and marketing communities, joining existing groups, and inserting itself into comparison-style content.

## Key Ideas

- Use social listening tools to find live discussions about competitor products and enter those conversations directly.
- Borrow distribution from places where the target audience already gathers instead of waiting to build your own following first.
- Use a lightweight comparison frame that positions the startup as a simpler or better-fit alternative to an incumbent.
- Seed the product across Hacker News, relevant subreddits, Facebook or LinkedIn groups, Pinterest boards, and community threads.
- Publish comparative "listicle" content that includes the startup inside a category the audience is already searching or discussing.
- Keep bios and DMs open so community participation can convert into inbound conversations.

## Claims Worth Tracking

- Manual demand capture through competitor mention monitoring may be one of the fastest ways to get early distribution without an existing audience.
- Borrowed-audience channels may outperform posting on the startup's own empty accounts during the earliest stage.
- Helpful educational participation inside adjacent groups may create qualified inbound interest if the pitch is restrained.
- Comparison-style posts and reviews may create a bridge between search intent, social discovery, and product consideration.

## Caveats

- Several tactics in the source can degrade into obvious spam if they are executed mechanically.
- Competitor-name replies only work if they are genuinely relevant, specific, and useful to the thread.
- Community promotion rules differ sharply across subreddits, HN, Facebook groups, LinkedIn groups, and Pinterest boards.
- Parasite marketing can damage reputation if it becomes extraction rather than contribution.
- The paid bundle CTA at the end signals that the post is also a funnel asset, not just neutral advice.
