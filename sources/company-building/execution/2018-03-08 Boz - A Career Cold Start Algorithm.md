# A Career Cold Start Algorithm

## What This Source Is

A short article describing a repeatable ramp-up method for entering a new team, role, or project without pretending to understand more than you do.

## Key Ideas

- Start with a 30-minute conversation that uses a strict 25/3/2 structure:
  - 25 minutes on "tell me everything you think I should know"
  - 3 minutes on the team's biggest current challenges
  - 2 minutes on who else you should talk to next
- Repeat the process recursively until people stop giving you new names.
- The first question does not give full understanding, but it gives a fast framework for the team's active topics, language, and context.
- The second question is a shortcut to early useful action because it reveals both structural problems and small neglected fixes.
- The third question produces an influence map that is often more accurate than the formal org chart.
- The act of asking, listening, and taking notes is itself trust-building behavior.

## Claims Worth Tracking

- Early impact often improves when a newcomer resists the urge to perform immediate expertise.
- Repeated interviews can surface the practical network, recurring pain points, and real decision centers of a team faster than formal documentation alone.
- Small operational annoyances may offer the fastest path to visible, positive contribution.

## Caveats

- This is a heuristic, not a substitute for months of deeper context building.
- The map you get will overrepresent whatever the current organization is actively talking about.
- The quality of the output depends on who is in the interview chain and how candid they are.
