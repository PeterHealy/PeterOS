# Agent-First Startups Tweet

## What This Source Is

A short X post from Greg Isenberg arguing that agent-first startups have a large open opportunity because incumbent SaaS platforms will become backends rather than the main product surface.

## Key Ideas

- The opportunity size is framed as extremely large and still open.
- Incumbent SaaS products are expected to become "headless" and act more like infrastructure or databases.
- New startups can win by making the agent the product instead of treating AI as a thin feature layer on top of traditional SaaS.

## Claims Worth Tracking

- "Agent-native" startups may be able to sit on top of systems like Salesforce, HubSpot, and Workday instead of competing by rebuilding their data layer from scratch.
- The user-facing surface may shift from dashboard-first software to agent-first workflows.
- Timing may matter: the source frames this as a temporary open window rather than a slow multi-year drift.

## Caveats

- This source is directional and promotional, not evidentiary.
- The accessible capture appears to be partial, so the summary is based only on the text that was retrievable.
- The thesis should be tested against counterarguments about trust, governance, auditability, and the continued need for visible control surfaces.
