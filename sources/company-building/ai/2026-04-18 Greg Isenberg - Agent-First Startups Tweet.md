# Agent-First Startups Tweet

## What This Source Is

A fuller user-provided capture of Greg Isenberg's April 18, 2026 X post arguing that agent-native startups will sit on top of incumbent SaaS, compress implementation-heavy services into software, and win through vertical workflow insight plus distribution.

## Key Ideas

- Incumbent SaaS systems such as Salesforce, HubSpot, and Workday may become system-of-record backends rather than the main product surface.
- The startup should make the agent the product and own the workflow end-to-end rather than adding a thin AI layer to a dashboard.
- Implementation and consulting work around enterprise SaaS may be compressible into software if the agent can execute the workflow directly.
- Outcome-based pricing may fit better than seat pricing when the agent is doing the work and the buyer only cares about business results.
- Founders with operator-grade knowledge of a specific vertical workflow may have an advantage over generalist builders.
- Distribution, brand, and audience may become the moat once basic agent wiring becomes commoditized.

## Claims Worth Tracking

- "Headless within 18 months" is a concrete timing claim that should be tested against actual vendor roadmaps and buyer behavior.
- The best early opportunities may be workflows still coordinated through dashboards, phone calls, spreadsheets, and service layers.
- Media plus software may become a repeatable distribution wedge for agent-native startups.
- The first big winners may have smaller teams, lower implementation cost, and higher margins than the SaaS products they displace.

## Caveats

- This source is directional and promotional, not evidentiary.
- Several claims depend on trust, governance, auditability, permissions, and exception handling that the post does not address.
- "No UX" is probably overstated; many categories will still need approval, visibility, and override surfaces even if the agent does most of the work.
