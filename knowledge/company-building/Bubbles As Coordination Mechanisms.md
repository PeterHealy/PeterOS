# Bubbles As Coordination Mechanisms

## Core Thesis

The best case for an inflection bubble is not that the financial speculation is wise. It is that a shared belief about the future coordinates many complementary investments at once, leaving behind physical capacity, cognitive capacity, and new market imagination even after the financial bubble bursts.

## Two Kinds Of Residue

Productive bubbles can leave two kinds of durable residue.

- Physical capacity: long-lived assets that become cheaper to use after speculative owners lose money, such as telecom fiber after the dotcom bust.
- Cognitive capacity: users, workers, protocols, tools, habits, and adjacent inventions created because many people were building toward the same future at the same time.

This matters because the bankrupt investor and the future user are not the same actor. The investor can lose money while the economy inherits useful capacity.

## Coordination Logic

Inflection bubbles reduce ecosystem risk by making complementary bets feel more plausible.

- If infrastructure appears, applications become easier to justify.
- If applications appear, distribution and monetization layers become easier to justify.
- If distribution works, more users and workers learn the new medium.
- If many people learn the medium, new companies can hire, sell, and build faster.

The bubble works as uncoordinated coordination: no single planner has to specify the future, but the shared belief pulls capital and talent in a common direction.

## AI Bubble Question

The AI bubble's long-term value depends on what it leaves behind.

Weak residue:

- Short-lived GPUs.
- Overbuilt specialized datacenters that cannot be reused cheaply.
- Wrapper companies whose only asset was model access or market froth.

Strong residue:

- Power generation and grid capacity.
- Semiconductor fabs and manufacturing know-how.
- New chip architectures, lithography techniques, and cooling systems.
- AI-native workers, users, workflows, tools, and expectations.
- General-purpose infrastructure that future industries can use even if many AI companies fail.

## Power As The Key Residue

Power is an unusually important bubble residue because it is a general-purpose input. If AI demand forces massive new generation, transmission, and permitting reform, the payoff could extend beyond AI into any industry constrained by energy cost or availability.

The durable question is not only "will AI revenue justify the capex?" It is also "will the capex make energy cheap enough that new categories of invention become practical?"

## Evaluation Questions

- Is the bubble funding long-lived assets or mostly depreciating assets?
- If the leading companies fail, what useful infrastructure remains?
- Are complementary parts of the ecosystem becoming less risky because others are investing?
- Is the bubble training a new user base, workforce, or developer base?
- Which bottleneck becomes newly visible because demand has outrun the old system?
- Would the same infrastructure likely have been built without the mania?
- Does the bubble increase experimentation in genuinely adjacent stack layers, or just imitation at the application layer?
