# AI Automation And Job Displacement

## Core Model

AI exposure is not the same as displacement risk. A better first-pass model asks four questions:

1. Are the job's tasks separable or complementary?
2. Does automation free the worker to do the remaining tasks much better?
3. Will lower prices or better output expand demand enough to absorb higher productivity?
4. Is the job low-dimensional enough that automating one or two core tasks eliminates the role?

## Exposure Is Not Displacement

Exposure indices usually ask what share of a job's tasks can be affected by AI. That is useful for mapping where AI can enter work, but it does not answer whether workers are replaced.

Two jobs with the same exposure score can have opposite outcomes:

- AI may complement a high-dimensional job by clearing routine work and increasing the value of judgment, trust, taste, coordination, or client communication.
- AI may substitute for a low-dimensional job if one automatable bottleneck is close to the whole role.

## O-Ring Complementarity

Many jobs behave like O-ring systems: the output is valuable only when multiple tasks are done well together. In those jobs, task quality multiplies rather than merely adds.

This creates a focus effect. If AI automates some tasks at adequate quality, the human can concentrate more attention on the remaining tasks. The remaining-task improvements can multiply through the whole output, so partial automation can raise productivity and wages.

Examples:

- Doctors using AI for notes or diagnostic support while focusing on clinical judgment and patients.
- Research teams using AI for routine analysis while increasing the value of ideas, design, and interpretation.
- Consultants using AI for first-pass research or slides while spending more time on client trust, synthesis, and implementation.

## Demand Absorption

Higher productivity can still reduce employment if demand does not expand.

- Elastic demand: lower prices or better output create enough new demand that total work expands; employment can rise.
- Inelastic demand: output does not expand much, so firms need fewer workers to meet roughly the same demand.

This is the labor-market version of Jevons' paradox: efficiency can increase total use when demand is elastic, but it can reduce labor demand when the market is already saturated or constrained.

## Job Dimensionality

Job dimensionality is the number of core tasks that must remain human for the job to remain a job.

Low-dimensional jobs face two risks:

- Full displacement is closer because one or two automated tasks can remove the role.
- Firm incentives are stronger because a costly automation project can eliminate a wage bill, not just improve one activity.

This is why trucking and warehousing may be more exposed than many digital white-collar jobs. A management consultant may have many AI-exposed tasks and still remain necessary for judgment and trust. A truck driver may have lower measured AI exposure, but the core task of driving is a much larger share of the job.

## Practical Heuristic

When evaluating an occupation, do not ask only "can AI do these tasks?"

Ask:

- What is the small set of bottleneck tasks that makes the job still require a human?
- Are the remaining human tasks high-trust, high-context, high-judgment, or physically/regulatorily constrained?
- If productivity rises, is demand likely to expand more than proportionally?
- Would a firm pay the adoption cost because the automation removes a role, or only because it helps a worker?
- Could the job become low-dimensional later after supporting tasks are quietly automated elsewhere?

## Open Questions

- Which knowledge jobs look high-dimensional today but are gradually becoming low-dimensional as organizational systems absorb supporting work?
- Where is demand elastic enough that AI creates more hiring rather than fewer jobs?
- Which physical automation domains are closest to constrained-route reliability, regulatory approval, and insurance acceptance?
- How should exposure dashboards represent firm incentives and dimensionality without becoming too complex to read?
