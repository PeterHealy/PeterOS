## Raw Submitted Capture

The fastest way to make more money with your AI business is not more features, not more traffic, not more marketing.
It's fixing your pricing.
I built Vectal from nothing to $155,000 ARR. And if you look at the revenue chart, I was stuck below $2k MRR for over two months.
Just me, Claude, and Cursor -- no team, no employees, no investors.
I was sending a ton of traffic to Vectal and nothing was working. I couldn't break through that ceiling until I fixed one thing.
My pricing.
The Pricing Trap That Keeps You Broke
Here's what most first-time founders do -- they undercharge. That's it. That is the main problem. And how do they decide their pricing? They just copy what everybody else does.
That is stupid. And it will keep you poor.
The moment you think about raising your prices, your brain goes: "Will I lose customers? What if people won't pay this much for software? What if I scare people away?"
All of these are limiting beliefs.
Here's the truth -- you are not OpenAI. You cannot afford to burn tens of billions of dollars chasing market share. So why are you charging like OpenAI?
It's much easier to reach $20,000 a month with higher pricing. The math is just simpler. With $20 a month subscriptions, you need 1,000 unique paying clients. Let me tell you -- even with a big online audience, that is very hard to get.
What I Actually Did
At the start, my pricing was embarrassing. A free tier and a single $30 a month plan. Very bad, very basic, very amateur.
After I learned more about pricing, I made a few key changes. I kept the free plan. I dropped Pro to $20 (minor change). But the main thing? I added a more expensive tier at $100 a month. And the second main thing -- I got serious about annual plans.
That combination changed everything.
The Anchor Price Cheat Code
When you load Vectal's pricing page, it defaults to annual. That's intentional. And when you look at the tiers, that expensive $100 a month plan does something powerful -- it makes the Pro tier look super cheap.
This is called an anchor price. And here's the cheat code that's almost unfair -- you don't even need to launch the expensive tier. Just add it to your pricing page with the text "Coming Soon" and it will automatically make your main offer look way more affordable.
Let me repeat that. You don't need to build it. You don't need to sell it. Just put it there. And you will make more money.
Hormozi wrote a whole book on this (it's called the money model). I definitely recommend reading it. For some reason, people in the software space think these fundamental business principles don't apply to AI SaaS. All of them apply. And I'd argue there's even better arbitrage here because of so much ego -- everybody's in the Silicon Valley Y Combinator mindset and they forget that basic business is still basic business.
Annual Plans Are Insanely Underrated
A lot of you might have an annual plan. But are you actually selling it? Are you making it the main thing?
Here's what opened my eyes. I was talking to a friend who runs the second biggest AI newsletter. He explained something simple -- when you push the annual plan as the default, you unlock paid advertising entirely.
Look at it this way. Our Ultra plan is $100 a month. That's $1,200 per year. But we charge $800 per year instead -- which means you save $400, basically four months free. When a single annual Ultra customer pays $800 upfront, I can now spend up to $700 acquiring that customer through Facebook Ads, Google Ads, or influencer marketing and still be profitable day one.
Cash collected. Day one. Instantly in the profit.
Now compare that to pushing the $20 a month Pro plan. Good luck breaking even on any paid advertising with that. It's terrible. You simply cannot run paid acquisition on low-ticket monthly pricing.
This is a complete game changer -- not just increasing your prices, but pushing to annual.
Make Your Annual Plan Sexier
You need to sweeten the deal. Include personalized onboarding. Include some tech support. Yeah, these things aren't scalable. You probably don't want to do them forever. But that doesn't matter right now.
Your goal is to get to $20,000 a month. Get rich or die trying. That should be the mindset.
I don't care if you have to do some calls. I don't care if some customers are annoying and you spend time on support. That is better than being broke and not having money.
More cash up front gives you the budget to fund ads, grow faster, and create a positive loop that feeds itself.
Upsell Your Current Customers
"But David, what about my current customers?"
Simple. Grandfather their pricing. This means you don't charge them more -- you keep them on their current plan. They'll actually be happy you raised prices because they get to keep their old deal.
But here's the real play -- upsell your happy customers to the new higher plan. Credit them. If someone is paying $20 a month and wants to upgrade, make it $80 for them. Get clever about it.
The fastest way to make more money in any business -- AI software, brick and mortar, doesn't matter -- is to upsell something to current customers. That is infinitely easier than getting new customers. Just figure out other problems your current customers have and offer the solution. Simple as that.
And if you do this well, you can actually achieve negative churn. If more people upgrade and you keep increasing prices over time, you can offset the customers you lose. Let's say you lose 10% of customers per year (which would be amazing churn). If the remaining customers upgrade and spend 15% more with you, your company is growing by itself.
That's the dream.
Stop Overthinking API Costs
A lot of people freak out about this. "What models should I use? What if I go bankrupt?"
There are simple solutions. Different limits for different tiers. Free users don't get expensive models. Paid users get reasonable limits so you stay profitable. In the background, you can compact context windows, swap in cheaper models for background agents, use Gemini Flash for things the user doesn't see directly.
But here's the main mindset shift -- if you're spending too much on API calls, it means you're reaching product-market fit. That is a blessing. That is a good problem to have. Most people never get there.
Do not solve problems that haven't arised yet. This is not just a business principle -- it's a life principle. So many people worry about what happens with 1 million users and their API costs when they have 12 customers. That's absolutely stupid. Cross that bridge when you come to it. Focus on the biggest problem right in front of you right now.
The $300 a Month Framework
Get to $20K a month ASAP. And do it in the simplest way possible with as little friction as possible.
Get rid of the fancy ideas. Get rid of the limiting beliefs. Delete the incorrect assumptions.
Instead of going after everybody like ChatGPT does -- again, you are not OpenAI, you are not a billion dollar company -- pick a tiny niche. Build a unique AI agent for that niche. Then dominate it. (This is from the book Zero to One -- also highly recommend.)
Don't be afraid to charge $300 a month. If you're solving a painful problem for a business or a wealthy individual with a unique product, it should be worth way more than that. And charge a setup fee -- something like $3,000 up front, then $300 a month after. This also lowers your churn massively, because somebody who pays $3K isn't canceling over $300 a month.
What does that math look like? You only need 7 new clients per month to hit $20,000 a month.
Compare that to charging $20 a month where you need 1,000 unique paying clients. Way, way, way more difficult.
Just listen to the advice. It comes from mistakes I made and a lot of time I wasted myself.
