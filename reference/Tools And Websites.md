# Tools And Websites

## What This Page Is

A shared registry for concrete tools, websites, APIs, extensions, and products that come up in notes or source ingests. Keep entries short: URL, what the thing is for, and where it came from.

## Company Building

- [Varda Space Industries](https://www.varda.com/) - space manufacturing company using recoverable capsules for pharmaceutical manufacturing and hypersonic reentry services. Mentioned in [[sources/reading-writing/blogs-essays-articles/Packy McCormick - Varda The Space Drug Factory]].
- [GitLab Handbook](https://handbook.gitlab.com/) - public company operating handbook; useful as a reference for radical transparency, remote operating systems, and documentation-heavy org design. Mentioned in [[sources/reading-writing/blogs-essays-articles/Elliot Hershberg - Going Founder Mode On Cancer]].
- [Kilo Code](https://kilo.ai/) - open-source AI coding agent platform associated with Sid Sijbrandij; useful for tracking founder/operator work referenced in the source. Mentioned in [[sources/reading-writing/blogs-essays-articles/Elliot Hershberg - Going Founder Mode On Cancer]].
- [Colossus](https://colossus.com/) - business, investing, and company-profile publication and podcast network; useful as a source of long-form profiles and market structure essays. Mentioned in [[sources/company-building/execution/Dom Cooke - Beyond the Sky]].
- [Hyperliquid](https://hyperliquid.xyz/) - decentralized exchange and blockchain focused on perpetual futures and public financial infrastructure. Mentioned in [[sources/company-building/execution/Dom Cooke - Beyond the Sky]].
- [Clay](https://www.clay.com/) - GTM data, enrichment, intent, and AI workflow platform; useful for research, segmentation, enrichment, and outbound systems. Mentioned in [[sources/company-building/marketing/Alex Lieberman - Claude Code Replaced My 20-Person Marketing Team]].
- [Graphed](https://www.graphed.com/) - AI marketing platform with agent, dashboard, and templated page workflows; relevant here because the notes reference Graphed-style ad and landing-page generation. Mentioned in [[sources/company-building/marketing/Alex Lieberman - Claude Code Replaced My 20-Person Marketing Team]].
- [HeyGen](https://www.heygen.com/) - AI video generation and localization tool; useful for scaling up winning creative into faster video production. Mentioned in [[sources/company-building/marketing/Alex Lieberman - Claude Code Replaced My 20-Person Marketing Team]].
- [Keywords Everywhere](https://keywordseverywhere.com/) - browser-based keyword and SERP research tool; useful for turning search demand into landing-page and blog-post ideas. Mentioned in [[sources/company-building/marketing/Alex Lieberman - Claude Code Replaced My 20-Person Marketing Team]].
- [Apollo](https://www.apollo.io/) - lead enrichment and sales engagement platform; useful for turning social engagers into contactable prospects. Mentioned in [[sources/company-building/marketing/Alex Lieberman - Claude Code Replaced My 20-Person Marketing Team]].
- [Google Search Console](https://search.google.com/search-console) - search performance and indexing console; useful for sitemap submission, coverage monitoring, and page inspection. Mentioned in [[sources/company-building/marketing/Alex Lieberman - Claude Code Replaced My 20-Person Marketing Team]].
- [Google Indexing API](https://developers.google.com/search/apis/indexing-api/v3/using-api) - API for notifying Google about certain page updates and removals; official docs say it is limited to job posting and livestream pages, so use it carefully. Mentioned in [[sources/company-building/marketing/Alex Lieberman - Claude Code Replaced My 20-Person Marketing Team]].
- [Substack](https://substack.com/) - publishing, email, subscription, and community platform; relevant as the infrastructure layer Rachitsky used to turn expert writing into a paid media business. Mentioned in [[sources/company-building/execution/Nate Martins - Reluctantly Influential]].
- [Lenny's Product Pass](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/productpass) - bundle of software offers for Lenny's Newsletter paid subscribers; useful as an example of value-add bundling around a trusted audience. Mentioned in [[sources/company-building/execution/Nate Martins - Reluctantly Influential]].
- [Lenny's Data](https://www.lennysdata.com/) - AI-friendly Markdown archive of Lenny's Newsletter and podcast transcripts, plus related MCP/GitHub infrastructure; useful as a reference for making a content archive usable by LLMs. Mentioned in [[sources/company-building/execution/Nate Martins - Reluctantly Influential]].

## AI Agents And Robotics

- [Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/) - AI company behind Claude; relevant to AI-risk, alignment, transparency, and frontier-model governance sources. Mentioned in [[sources/reading-writing/blogs-essays-articles/Dario Amodei - The Adolescence of Technology]].
- [Letters from a Zeneca](https://www.zeneca.xyz/) - newsletter covering crypto, AI agents, Claude Code, and practical local-LLM workflows. Mentioned in [[sources/ai-agents/Zeneca - All About Local LLMs]].
- [LM Studio](https://lmstudio.ai/) - desktop app for downloading, testing, and serving local LLMs with a GUI and OpenAI-compatible API. Mentioned in [[sources/ai-agents/Zeneca - All About Local LLMs]].
- [Ollama](https://ollama.com/) - local model runtime and background service for running LLMs through terminal commands and local API endpoints. Mentioned in [[sources/ai-agents/Zeneca - All About Local LLMs]].
- [Ollama Library](https://ollama.com/library) - catalog of models packaged for Ollama. Mentioned in [[sources/ai-agents/Zeneca - All About Local LLMs]].
- [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/) - model, dataset, and AI artifact hub; useful as the broader model repository behind many local-model downloads. Mentioned in [[sources/ai-agents/Zeneca - All About Local LLMs]].
- [Unsloth](https://unsloth.ai/) - tooling for fine-tuning open models on local or custom data. Mentioned in [[sources/ai-agents/Zeneca - All About Local LLMs]].
- [llama.cpp](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp) - low-level local LLM inference engine used by many local model tools. Mentioned in [[sources/ai-agents/Zeneca - All About Local LLMs]].
- [MLX](https://github.com/ml-explore/mlx) - Apple machine-learning framework relevant to local inference on Apple Silicon. Mentioned in [[sources/ai-agents/Zeneca - All About Local LLMs]].
- [General Intuition](https://www.generalintuition.com/) - world-model and embodied-AI company building action-conditioned models and agents; central company thesis in [[sources/ai-agents/Packy McCormick and Pim De Witte - World Models Computing the Uncomputable]].
- [Medal](https://medal.tv/) - gaming clip platform with action-labeled clips; relevant as a potential world-model training data source in [[sources/ai-agents/Packy McCormick and Pim De Witte - World Models Computing the Uncomputable]].
- [World Labs](https://www.worldlabs.ai/) - Fei-Fei Li's spatial-intelligence company; useful for tracking world-model and 3D/spatial AI work. Mentioned in [[sources/ai-agents/Packy McCormick and Pim De Witte - World Models Computing the Uncomputable]].
- [AMI Labs](https://amilabs.xyz/) - advanced machine intelligence lab associated with Yann LeCun's world-model approach. Mentioned in [[sources/ai-agents/Packy McCormick and Pim De Witte - World Models Computing the Uncomputable]].
- [World Models interactive paper](https://worldmodels.github.io/) - interactive version of Ha and Schmidhuber's 2018 `World Models` paper; useful for understanding the vision-memory-controller architecture. Mentioned in [[sources/ai-agents/Packy McCormick and Pim De Witte - World Models Computing the Uncomputable]].
- [openpilot](https://github.com/commaai/openpilot) - comma.ai's open-source driver assistance stack; relevant because the source discusses deployment of a world-model-trained policy in openpilot. Mentioned in [[sources/ai-agents/Packy McCormick and Pim De Witte - World Models Computing the Uncomputable]].
