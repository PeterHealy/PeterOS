# AI SaaS Pricing Checklist

## Use When

Use this before changing pricing for a small AI software business, especially when the product has traffic, users, or early customers but revenue is stuck.

## Quick Checks

- Is the product priced like a mass-market consumer AI tool even though it serves a niche or business workflow?
- Does the current price require hundreds or thousands of customers to reach meaningful MRR?
- Is there a serious-user tier that makes the core plan look affordable?
- Is annual billing the default or at least strongly presented?
- Does the annual plan include enough value to justify upfront payment?
- Are expensive models and high-usage behaviors limited by tier?
- Is there an upgrade path for happy existing customers?
- Are old customers grandfathered if prices rise?
- Is manual onboarding/support priced, tracked, and used for learning?

## Useful Pricing Moves

- Add a high anchor tier above the main plan.
- Make annual billing visually and commercially prominent.
- Add onboarding, support, setup, or configuration to the annual or high tier.
- Add usage/model limits so free and low-tier users cannot consume the margin.
- Create an upgrade offer for current happy customers rather than only acquiring new customers.
- Consider a setup fee for narrow B2B agents that require workflow configuration.

## Red Flags

- The product is too generic to justify a premium.
- The high tier is fake, vague, or never intended to ship.
- Paid acquisition is being tested against low-ticket monthly plans with no day-one payback path.
- API costs are being optimized before there is meaningful demand.
- Manual support is bundled into cheap plans with no margin model.
- The pricing page hides annual billing even though upfront cash would materially improve growth options.
