# Startup Promotion Without an Audience

## What It Is

A compact reference distilled from a March 2026 post about promoting a startup from zero followers. The durable idea is to borrow existing attention where the target audience already is, but do it in a way that preserves trust.

## Tactics Worth Testing

- Monitor competitor mentions and category keywords so you can show up where buyer intent already exists.
- Join communities, groups, and threads where the ICP already spends time rather than only posting from your own empty accounts.
- Lead with useful educational replies that solve small concrete problems before mentioning the product.
- Use comparison posts, category roundups, and review-style content to place the startup into an existing buying frame.
- Make conversion paths easy: clear bio, clear positioning, and open DMs or another low-friction contact method.
- Treat early distribution as manual demand capture, not as abstract "brand building."

## Tactics To Treat Carefully

- Dropping your product into competitor threads with templated comments.
- Posting the same startup link across many subreddits or groups without adapting to context.
- Using "parasite marketing" as extraction rather than contribution.
- Assuming every borrowed-audience channel fits every startup.
- Buying into growth bundles or tool stacks before proving one repeatable acquisition loop.

## Practical Operating Model

- Pick a narrow ICP and a narrow problem.
- Identify the communities, search patterns, and competitor mentions that reveal active demand.
- Show up with useful answers, examples, and comparisons that match the local context.
- Mention the product only when it clearly fits the conversation.
- Track downstream signals such as profile clicks, DMs, demo requests, trials, or retained users.
- Stop using channels where the startup consistently gets low-trust reactions or low-quality users.

## Channels Mentioned In The Source

- competitor mention monitoring via social listening tools
- Hacker News
- subreddits
- Facebook groups
- LinkedIn groups
- Pinterest group boards
- Reddit listicles and comparison posts

## Business Translation

- The real asset is not "being everywhere."
- The real asset is finding where intent already exists and entering those conversations without breaking trust.
- A small number of high-fit community surfaces usually beats spraying links widely.
