# Ben Alistor Content Rules

## What It Is

A short content quality filter built around three questions to ask before posting.

## The Three Rules

1. Could anyone else say this?
If yes, redo it and add a clearer point of view.

2. Am I talking at people or with people?
If it feels like a broadcast instead of a conversation, it is probably weak.

3. Does the hook lead with the outcome?
The audience should know what they are about to consume and why it matters.

## Why Keep It

- It is compact enough to use as a pre-publish checklist.
- It catches three common failure modes fast:
  - generic takes
  - preachy tone
  - vague or low-clarity hooks

## How To Use This

- Run a draft through all three questions before posting.
- If rule 1 fails, sharpen the point of view.
- If rule 2 fails, rewrite toward dialogue and specificity.
- If rule 3 fails, make the promised value obvious in the hook.

## Practical Interpretation

- Distinctiveness: avoid content that could have come from anyone.
- Conversation: write like there is a smart person on the other side, not like you are delivering a lecture.
- Outcome clarity: tell people what they will get, learn, feel, or avoid by continuing.
