# Career Cold Start Algorithm

## What It Is

A simple ramp-up procedure for entering a new role, team, or project and building context quickly without faking expertise.

## Core Loop

1. Find someone on the team and ask for 30 minutes.
2. Spend 25 minutes asking them to tell you everything they think you should know.
3. Spend 3 minutes asking about the biggest challenges right now.
4. Spend 2 minutes asking who else you should talk to.
5. Repeat with every new name until no new names appear.

## Why It Works

- It gives you a fast mental framework for the work before you understand the full system.
- It reveals recurring pain points and the team's current active concerns.
- It teaches you the team's terminology, assumptions, and hidden context.
- It produces a map of influence that is often more useful than the org chart.
- Listening first signals respect and builds trust.

## Practical Interpretation

- Use this when starting a new job, joining a new team, or cold-starting on a new project.
- Treat repeated names and repeated problems as high-signal data.
- Look for small fixable annoyances alongside large strategic issues.
- Convert your notes into three lists:
  - people to understand
  - problems to watch
  - easy wins to act on

## Failure Modes

- Talking too much instead of listening.
- Pretending to understand terminology you do not understand.
- Stopping the interview chain too early.
- Chasing only the most glamorous problems instead of the small recurring drags on execution.
